# Assumption-Evidence Table

**Course:** ICT105 | **Team:** Lemon | **Based on:** 20 survey responses

| No. | Team Assumption | Evidence Collected | Evidence Type | Strength | Decision |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | International and first-year students struggle most with finding campus information. | 14 out of 20 respondents are first-year or international students. All rated difficulty 4 or 5. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 2 | Information about campus life is too scattered across different channels. | 8 out of 20 selected 'Information is scattered' as their biggest problem — the most common answer. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 3 | Students spend more than 30 minutes searching for basic campus information. | 13 out of 20 spent 30 minutes or more. 7 spent more than 1 hour. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 4 | New students rate the difficulty of finding campus information as 4 or 5 out of 5. | 11 out of 20 rated difficulty as 5. 6 rated it as 4. Only 3 rated it as 2 — all had been at university more than 6 months. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 5 | Dorm and cafeteria reviews are the most needed feature for new students. | Dorm reviews selected by 7 respondents, cafeteria reviews by 5. First-week guide also received 5 votes — nearly equal. | Survey | Moderate | Revise |
| 6 | Language barrier is a major problem for international and exchange students. | All 4 international and 2 exchange students selected 'Hard to find in my language' as their main pain point. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 7 | Most students would be willing to use HallPass if it existed. | 16 out of 20 said Yes. 4 said Maybe. None said No. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 8 | Feeling stressed is the main consequence of not finding campus information. | 8 out of 20 reported stress. 6 reported wasting time. 3 reported making a bad decision. Stress is top but not by a large margin. | Survey | Moderate | Revise |

## Evidence Strength Guide
- Weak: team opinion or friend feedback only
- Moderate: repeated interview pattern from target users
- Strong: users show behavior, complete a task, sign up, or provide specific evidence

## Summary
6 out of 8 assumptions are Strong and marked Keep. The remaining 2 are Moderate and marked Revise — the first-week guide should be added alongside dorm and cafeteria reviews as an equal priority feature, and the consequence section should be expanded to cover both stress and wasted time.
