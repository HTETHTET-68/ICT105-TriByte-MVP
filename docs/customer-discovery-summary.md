# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title
HallPass — University Survival App for International Students

## Target Users Studied
International students, exchange students, and first-year students at university, particularly those in their first semester who are unfamiliar with campus facilities and daily life routines.

## Number of Responses / Interviews
20 survey responses collected via Google Forms.

## Main Repeated Problem
Students — especially international, exchange, and first-year students — cannot find reliable and honest information about dorms and cafeterias in one place. Information is scattered across group chats, social media, and the university website, and is rarely written by real students.

## Main Current Workarounds
- Asking friends or classmates
- Posting in LINE or WhatsApp group chats
- Searching on social media such as Facebook or Instagram
- Checking the university website
- Walking in and figuring things out on their own

## Strongest Evidence Found
- 14 out of 20 respondents are first-year, international, or exchange students and all rated difficulty as 4 or 5 out of 5.
- 13 out of 20 spent more than 30 minutes searching for basic campus information.
- 8 out of 20 identified scattered information as their biggest problem — the most common single answer.
- 16 out of 20 said they would use HallPass if it existed.
- All international and exchange students flagged language as a major barrier and selected multilingual content as the most useful feature.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings
- It is unclear whether students would actively write reviews or only read them. No question directly measured willingness to contribute content regularly.
- The first-week guide received as many votes as cafeteria reviews, but we did not ask which feature students would use most frequently.
- Students who said Maybe to using HallPass did not explain their hesitation clearly enough to act on.

## What We Must Revise
- Add the first-week guide as an equal priority feature alongside dorm and cafeteria reviews — not a secondary feature.
- Expand the consequence section to cover both stress and wasted time, not just stress alone.
- In the next round of discovery, ask whether students would write reviews or only read them to understand content contribution behavior.

## Decision for Next Lab
- Continue with same problem direction

## Updated Problem Statement
International, exchange, and first-year university students have no single reliable place to find honest, student-written information about dorms, cafeterias, and first-week essentials. As a result, they spend over 30 minutes searching across scattered channels, often arriving at decisions with incomplete or outdated information, leading to stress and wasted time during an already difficult transition period. For international and exchange students, the challenge is made worse by a language barrier that makes existing resources even harder to use.
